Bengaluru

Bengaluru, also known as Bangalore, is a city in the southern Indian state of Karnataka. It is known for its pleasant climate, vibrant culture, and technological prowess. Here are some places that are worth visiting in Bengaluru:
Lalbagh Botanical Garden: This 240-acre garden is home to a wide variety of plants, trees, and flowers. It features a glasshouse modeled after London's Crystal Palace and is a popular spot for picnics and photography.
Cubbon Park: This 300-acre park is a green oasis in the heart of the city. It features a large collection of trees, a bandstand, and a number of statues and monuments.
Bangalore Palace: This palace was built in the 19th century by the Wodeyar dynasty and features a mix of Tudor and Scottish Gothic architecture. It is now a popular venue for concerts and events.
Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace: This palace was built by the famous Mysore ruler Tipu Sultan in the late 18th century. It features teakwood pillars and arches and is a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture.
ISKCON Temple: This temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, is a popular pilgrimage site for devotees of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness. It features beautiful architecture, a vegetarian restaurant, and a gift shop.
Bangalore Fort: This 16th-century fort was originally built by Kempe Gowda, the founder of Bangalore. It was later modified by Hyder Ali and his son Tipu Sultan, and features a number of historic landmarks and monuments.
Wonderla Amusement Park: This amusement park is a great place for families and thrill-seekers. It features a variety of rides, water slides, and attractions.
Nandi Hills: This scenic hill station is located about 60 km from Bangalore and is a popular spot for weekend getaways. It features a number of temples, viewpoints, and hiking trails.
